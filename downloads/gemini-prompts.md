# Gemini Prompt Cheat Sheet — High School Math Teachers
*LIFT LAB Workshop | March 17, 2026 | CSUDH*

**How to use this sheet:** Copy a prompt, fill in the [BRACKETS], paste into Gemini
**Gemini's superpower:** Lives inside Google — works with your Classroom, Docs, Slides, and Forms
**Free with your school Google account at:** https://gemini.google.com

---

## WHAT GEMINI IS BEST FOR

| Task | Why Gemini |
|------|-----------|
| Rubrics in Google Classroom | Generates and drops rubric directly into Classroom |
| Lesson slides | Creates slides directly in Google Slides |
| Assignment descriptions | Drafts inside Google Docs |
| Standards-tagged content | Links content to CA Common Core standards |
| Google Forms quizzes | Helps build quiz questions in Forms |
| Summarizing class progress | Uses your actual Classroom data |

## SECTION A: GEMINI AT GEMINI.GOOGLE.COM

### 1. LESSON PLAN (Google Docs-ready)

> "Create a [LENGTH]-minute lesson plan for [GRADE] [SUBJECT] on [TOPIC]. Format it so I can paste it directly into a Google Doc. Include: learning objective, materials needed, warm-up, instruction, guided practice, independent practice, and closing. Align to California Common Core [STANDARD if known]."

### 2. PARENT NEWSLETTER DRAFT

> "Write a parent newsletter for my [GRADE] [SUBJECT] class. This month we're covering [TOPIC 1] and [TOPIC 2]. The newsletter should: explain what we're learning in plain language, mention our upcoming [TEST/PROJECT] on [DATE], give parents one practical tip to help at home, and be friendly and brief (under 200 words). Format it so it's ready to paste into a Google Doc."

### 3. ASSIGNMENT DESCRIPTION

> "Write a clear assignment description for a [GRADE] [SUBJECT] class. The assignment is: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. Include: what students will do, what to turn in, how it will be graded (brief), and the due date [DATE]. Keep it concise and student-friendly."

### 4. QUIZ QUESTIONS

> "Create [NUMBER] quiz questions for a [GRADE] [SUBJECT] quiz on [TOPIC]. Include [X] multiple choice (with 4 options each), [X] short answer, and [X] true/false. Provide an answer key. Make sure the difficulty is appropriate for [GRADE] students."

### 5. STANDARDS ALIGNMENT

> "I'm teaching [TOPIC] to [GRADE] students. Which California Common Core Math standards does this topic address? List the standard codes and descriptions. Then suggest 3 specific learning objectives I can write for this lesson."

## SECTION B: GEMINI IN GOOGLE CLASSROOM

*Access via: Google Classroom → Create Assignment → click the Gemini icon*

### 6. RUBRIC GENERATOR (inside Classroom)

> "Create a rubric for a [GRADE] [SUBJECT] [ASSIGNMENT TYPE] on [TOPIC]. Use 4 performance levels: Exceeds Standards, Meets Standards, Approaching Standards, Below Standards. Evaluate these criteria: [CRITERION 1], [CRITERION 2], [CRITERION 3]."

**Steps:**
1. Open Google Classroom
2. Create or open an assignment
3. Click "Rubric" → "Generate rubric with AI"
4. Describe your assignment → Gemini builds the rubric
5. Edit as needed → Attach to assignment

### 7. ASSIGNMENT CREATION (inside Classroom)

> "Create a [SUBJECT] assignment for [GRADE] students on [TOPIC]. The assignment should take about [TIME] to complete. Include: a student-friendly description, clear instructions, and what students need to submit."

## SECTION C: GEMINI IN GOOGLE DOCS

*Access via: Google Docs → Help me write (pen icon) or Insert → Help me write*

### 8. LESSON NOTES / TEACHER GUIDE

Highlight your rough notes in a Google Doc and ask Gemini:
> "Help me turn these rough notes into a clear, organized teacher guide for a lesson on [TOPIC]."

Or start fresh:
> "Write organized teacher notes for a [GRADE] [SUBJECT] lesson on [TOPIC]. Include: key vocabulary with definitions, step-by-step teaching sequence, anticipated student questions and answers, and a quick formative assessment idea."

### 9. EMAIL / LETTER DRAFTING

> "Draft a professional email to parents/guardians about [SITUATION — e.g., 'upcoming state testing', 'low test scores', 'a field trip opportunity', 'extra tutoring resources']. Keep it warm, brief, and action-oriented."

## SECTION D: GEMINI IN GOOGLE SLIDES

*Access via: Google Slides → Insert → Help me create a slide / Help me visualize*

### 10. SLIDE DECK CREATION

> "Create a slide deck for a [LENGTH]-minute [GRADE] [SUBJECT] lesson on [TOPIC]. Include slides for: title, learning objectives, vocabulary, step-by-step worked example, practice problems, and exit ticket. Keep slides clean with minimal text — use bullet points, not paragraphs."

### 11. VISUAL EXPLANATION SLIDE

> "Create a slide that visually explains [MATH CONCEPT] for [GRADE] students. Use a diagram, color-coding, or step-by-step visual breakdown. Keep text minimal."

## SECTION E: GEMINI IN GOOGLE FORMS

### 12. QUIZ / ASSESSMENT CREATION

*Use Gemini to brainstorm questions, then build in Google Forms manually or via Forms AI features*

> "Give me 10 multiple-choice questions for a Google Forms quiz on [TOPIC] for [GRADE] students. Include 4 answer options for each, mark the correct answer, and vary the difficulty (4 easy, 4 medium, 2 hard)."

## TIPS FOR GETTING THE BEST FROM GEMINI

**Tip 1 — Use Gemini where you already work:**
The power of Gemini is that it's IN Google Docs, Slides, Classroom, Forms. You don't have to copy-paste — it drafts directly in your document.

**Tip 2 — Give it your class context:**
Start conversations with: "I'm a [SUBJECT] teacher at a California high school. My students are [GRADE]." Gemini will use that context for the whole conversation.

**Tip 3 — Iterate in the same document:**
In Google Docs, you can ask Gemini to revise what it just wrote: "Make this shorter" / "Add more detail about [TOPIC]" / "Rewrite this for a struggling reader."

**Tip 4 — Verify math outputs:**
Gemini can make errors on complex calculations. Always check numerical answers before using in class.

**Tip 5 — Free for your school:**
If your school uses Google Workspace for Education, Gemini features are included at no cost. Check with your IT department if you don't see the Gemini icon in your Google apps.

**Tip 6 — Standards alignment:**
Always add "aligned to California Common Core" to any content generation prompt. Gemini will tag your content to the appropriate standards.
