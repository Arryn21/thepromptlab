    Real AI Outputs for Math Teachers

    Real prompts with real AI-generated results — use these as templates in your classroom.

    Worksheet ChatGPT
    Prompt Used
    Act as an experienced 9th grade Algebra teacher. Create a 10-question worksheet on solving two-step equations. Include space for student work and a separate answer key. All answers should be integers.
    AI Output (excerpt)
    Name: _______ Date: _______ Period: _____ Two-Step Equations Practice Directions: Solve each equation. Show all work. 1) 2x + 5 = 13 x = ___ 2) 3y – 7 = 14 y = ___ 3) –4n + 9 = –3 n = ___ [...10 problems total] Answer Key: 1) x=4 2) y=7 3) n=3 ...
    Explanation Claude
    Prompt Used
    Explain slope to a 9th grader who struggles with fractions. Use a real-world analogy they would relate to. Keep it under 150 words.
    AI Output
    Think of slope like a ramp at a skatepark. If the ramp rises 3 feet for every 4 feet you move forward, the slope is 3/4 — that's rise over run. A steeper ramp = bigger slope number. A flat surface = slope of 0. A ramp going downhill = negative slope. In math, slope tells you: "for every 1 step right, how many steps up (or down) do I go?" That's it. Rise ÷ Run. Steep hill vs. gentle hill.
    Differentiation Claude
    Prompt Used
    Create an explanation of the Pythagorean theorem for three reading levels: below grade level (5th grade reading), on grade level (9th grade), and advanced (honors). Same concept, different depth.
    AI Output (excerpt)
    Below Level: In a right triangle, the two shorter sides have a special relationship with the longest side. If you square each short side and add them, you get the square of the longest side: a² + b² = c². On Level: The Pythagorean theorem states that for any right triangle with legs a and b and hypotenuse c: a² + b² = c². This lets us find any missing side. Advanced: The theorem generalizes beyond Euclidean geometry — in non-Euclidean spaces, this relationship changes, forming the basis of Einstein's spacetime metric...
    Parent Email ChatGPT
    Prompt Used
    Write a professional, warm parent email explaining that a student is struggling with the quadratic unit. Suggest 2 concrete ways the parent can help at home. Keep it under 200 words.
    AI Output
    Dear [Parent/Guardian], I'm reaching out because I want to make sure [Student] has the support they need in our current unit on quadratic functions. While [Student] shows great effort in class, they're having difficulty connecting the algebraic process to the visual graph. Here's how you can help: 1. Khan Academy: Search "quadratic functions" — 10 min/night of practice videos makes a real difference. 2. Ask them to teach you: Have them explain what a parabola is using the word "vertex." If they can explain it simply, they understand it. I'm available during office hours (Tue/Thu 3–4 PM) and happy to schedule a call. Warm regards, [Your Name]
    Lesson Plan ChatGPT
    Prompt Used
    Design a 60-minute lesson plan for teaching the Pythagorean theorem to 9th graders. Include warm-up, direct instruction, guided practice, and an exit ticket. Align to CCSS 8.G.B.7.
    AI Output (excerpt)
    Standard: CCSS 8.G.B.7 — Apply the Pythagorean theorem Warm-Up (8 min): Show a right triangle on screen. Ask: "What's special about this triangle?" Direct Instruction (15 min): Introduce a² + b² = c² with a visual proof using squares drawn on each side... Guided Practice (20 min): Partner work — 6 problems increasing in difficulty... Exit Ticket (5 min): "A ladder 10 ft long leans against a wall. The base is 6 ft from the wall. How high does the ladder reach?"

    Breakout Room Activities

    1
    ChatGPT ⏱ 30 min

    Prompt Makeover

    Fix a bad prompt using the RTFC framework. Show your group the before and after — see how much better the output gets.

    • Start with this weak prompt: "Make a worksheet on algebra"
    • Use the RTFC builder to add Role, Task, Format, and Criteria
    • Send both versions to ChatGPT — compare the outputs side-by-side
    • Note 3 specific improvements in the second output
    Share Back to Room
    • Your original weak prompt
    • Your improved RTFC prompt
    • The biggest quality difference you noticed
    30:00
    2
    Claude ⏱ 30 min

    Differentiation Challenge

    Create the same content at 3 reading levels for one topic your group chooses. One prompt, three audiences.

    • Group picks a math concept (e.g., slope, fractions, quadratics)
    • Use Claude to generate: below grade level, on grade, and honors versions
    • Compare: What changed? What stayed the same?
    • Try one follow-up: "Add a visual analogy to the below-level version"
    Share Back to Room
    • Your topic and the 3 versions
    • Which version surprised you most
    • One thing you'd change before using in class
    30:00
    3
    Gemini vs ChatGPT ⏱ 30 min

    AI Showdown

    Same prompt, two tools — see which one wins for your use case. You might be surprised.

    • Pick a prompt from the library (or write your own)
    • Send the exact same prompt to both ChatGPT and Gemini
    • Score each response: clarity, accuracy, classroom-readiness (1–5)
    • Try one Google Workspace-specific prompt in Gemini (rubric or slide outline)
    Share Back to Room
    • The prompt you used
    • Which tool "won" and why
    • When you'd pick each tool in real life

    Math Viz Challenge

    Describe a math concept in plain English → AI Desmos Studio builds an interactive graph → share it with students.

    • Click "Copy AI Prompt" → paste into Claude → copy the JSON back → click "Load into Desmos"
    • Try a second, more complex graph: unit circle, trig comparison, or statistical plot
    Share Back to Room
