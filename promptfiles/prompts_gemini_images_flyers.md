## HOW TO REQUEST AN IMAGE FROM GEMINI

Basic format:
> "Create an image of [WHAT] in [STYLE] with [COLORS/MOOD]. It should include [TEXT/ELEMENTS]. Make it suitable for [PURPOSE]."

The more specific you are, the better the result. After you get an image, you can:
- Say **"Make it more colorful"** or **"Make it darker/lighter"**
- Say **"Add [element]"** or **"Remove [element]"**
- Say **"Try a different style"** (comic, watercolor, minimalist, photorealistic)
- Download the image and paste it into Canva, Google Slides, or Docs

## 1. CLASSROOM POSTERS

### Math Concept Poster
> "Create a colorful educational poster for a high school math classroom about [CONCEPT — e.g., 'the Pythagorean Theorem', 'slope-intercept form', 'order of operations']. Include a visual diagram, the key formula, and a simple real-world example. Style: bright, modern, engaging for teenagers. No people. Clean white background or gradient background."

### Motivational Math Poster
> "Create a motivational classroom poster for high school students with the quote '[QUOTE — e.g., Do Hard Math, Math is Everywhere, Struggle is Part of Learning]'. Make it visually striking with bold typography and a math-themed geometric pattern background. Colors: [YOUR SCHOOL COLORS or 'vibrant blues and yellows']. Style: modern and professional."

### Vocabulary / Formula Poster
> "Create an educational wall poster displaying the [TOPIC] formulas for a high school math classroom. Include: [FORMULA 1], [FORMULA 2], [FORMULA 3]. Use color-coding to highlight different parts of each formula. Style: clean, minimalist, easy to read from across the room. White or light background."

## 2. EVENT FLYERS

### Math Tutoring Flyer
> "Create a professional flyer advertising FREE math tutoring at [SCHOOL NAME] high school. Include space for: days and times, room number, and a contact email. Style: energetic, welcoming, appealing to high school students. Use bold colors. Add math symbols and geometric shapes as decoration. Leave clear areas where I can add specific text details."

### Math Club Flyer
> "Create an eye-catching flyer for a high school Math Club. The flyer should look fun and exciting — not intimidating. Include decorative math symbols (∑, π, √, ∞, ÷). Style: modern, colorful, appealing to teens aged 14-18. Leave space at the bottom for meeting time/location details. Horizontal format."

### Pi Day Event Flyer
> "Create a festive flyer for a Pi Day celebration (March 14) at a high school. Include the Pi symbol (π) prominently, the number 3.14159..., and imagery suggesting a fun school event. Make it colorful and celebratory. Style: festive but professional. Leave space for event details."

### Academic Challenge / Math Competition Flyer
> "Create a dramatic, exciting flyer for a high school math competition. Style: like a sports event poster — bold, high-energy, competitive. Include: calculator imagery, trophy imagery, and math symbols. Color scheme: bold and striking (e.g., navy and gold, or red and black). Leave areas for: competition name, date, how to sign up."

## 3. CLASSROOM HANDOUT HEADERS / BANNERS

### Worksheet Header Banner
> "Create a decorative header banner for a high school math worksheet on [TOPIC]. It should be horizontal (wide and short — like a header strip). Include: the topic name '[TOPIC]' in bold text, math-themed geometric decoration, and space for Name/Date/Period. Colors: [COLOR PREFERENCE]. Style: clean and professional, not too childish."

It should look like a textbook chapter opener — visually representing the concept with geometric shapes, graphs, or relevant imagery. Include the unit title '[TOPIC]' in large, clear text. ## REFINING YOUR IMAGE: FOLLOW-UP PROMPTS

After Gemini gives you an image, you can say:

| What you want | What to say |
|---|---|
| More colorful | "Make the colors more vibrant and eye-catching" |
| More professional | "Make it look more professional and polished" |
| Different style | "Redo this in a comic book style" / "watercolor style" / "minimalist style" |
| Add something | "Add a graduation cap to the image" |
| Change colors | "Change the color scheme to blue and gold" |
| Different mood | "Make it feel more fun and less formal" |
| Better for print | "Make it suitable for printing in black and white" |
| More space for text | "Add a large blank area at the bottom where I can add text" |

## TIPS FOR GETTING THE BEST IMAGES

**Tip 1 — Be specific about style:**
Add one of these to any prompt: "photorealistic", "digital illustration", "flat design", "watercolor", "comic book style", "minimalist", "3D rendered", "infographic style"

**Tip 2 — Specify the purpose:**
Always say what you'll use it for: "for a classroom poster", "for a printed flyer", "for a presentation slide", "for a social media post"

**Tip 3 — Specify orientation:**
- **Landscape/horizontal** = presentation slides, flyer backgrounds
- **Portrait/vertical** = printed flyers, door signs, posters
- **Square** = social media, handout headers

**Tip 4 — Request text-free versions:**
Say "No text in the image" if you want to add your own text in Canva or Google Slides. AI-generated text in images is often misspelled.

**Tip 5 — Add to Canva for text:**
Download your Gemini image → upload to Canva → add your own text, logos, and details using Canva's tools. This gives you the best of both: AI-generated visuals + Canva's typography.

**Tip 6 — Color coordination:**
If you know your school colors (ask your principal or check your school's website), always include them: "Use [SCHOOL NAME]'s colors: [COLOR 1] and [COLOR 2]"
